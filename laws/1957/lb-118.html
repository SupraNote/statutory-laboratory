Laws 1967, LB 118, pp. 1092–1104 (appr. May 21, 1957).

Chapter 300

Creates Water Pollution Control Council

Legislative Bill 118

Introduced by John Aufenkamp, 2nd District; Charles F. Tvrdik, 7th District; Dwain Williams, 35th District, George Syas, 4th District

AN ACT relating to the control and abatement of pollution of the surface and underground waters of the state; to designate a state water pollution control agency; to create a state water pollution control council; to provide for the appointment of the members thereof and their terms of office and duties; to provide for hearings and the procedure therefor; to provide for the administration and enforcement of this act; to provide for appeals, counsel, and proceedings as prescribed; to provide what shall be taken into consideration by the council in making its decisions; to provide for a city or village sinking fund and for a transfer of any unused balance all as provided; to provide a savings clause; and to declare an emergency.

Original; Archived

Be it enacted by the people of the State of Nebraska,

Section 1

It is declared to be the public policy of the State of Nebraska to maintain reasonable standards of quality of the waters of the state consistent with the right of appropriation and the use of water thereunder in accordance with the laws of the state, with public health and public enjoyment thereof, the propagation and protection of fish, wild life, and aquatic life, and the agricultural and industrial development of the state.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3001 (Cum. Supp. 1970)

Sec. 2

For the purpose of this act unless the context otherwise requires:

(1) Pollution shall mean contamination or other alteration of the physical, chemical, or biological properties of any waters of the state or such discharge of any liquid, gaseous, or solid substance into any waters of the state to such extent or in such volume as will create a nuisance or render such waters harmful, detrimental, or injurious to public health, safety, or welfare;

(2) Wastes shall mean sewage, industrial waste, and all other liquid, gaseous, or solid substances which may pollute or tend to pollute any waters of the state;

(3) Sewerage system shall mean pipe lines, conduits, pumping stations, and force mains, and all other constructions, devices, appurtenances, and facilities used for collecting or conducting wastest to a point of treatment or ultimate disposal;

(4) Treatment works shall mean any plant, disposal field, lagoon, dam, pumping station, incinerator, or other works used for the purpose of treating, stabilizing, or holding wastes;

(5) Disposal system shall mean a system for disposing of wastes, and includes sewerage systems and treatment works;

(6) Waters of the state shall mean the waters of all streams, lakes, ponds, marshes, watercourses, waterways, wells, springs, and all other bodies or accumulations of water, surface and underground, natural or artificial, public or private which are contained within, flow through, or border upon this state or any portion thereof;

(7) Person shall mean the state, any municipality, political subdivision, institution, public or private corporation, individual, partnership, or other entity; and

(8) Department shall mean the Department of Health.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3002 (Cum. Supp. 1970)

Sec. 3

(1) There is hereby created within the department, a state water pollution control council, herein referred to as the council, which shall consist of nine members as follows:

(a) The Director of Health, the secretary of the Game, Forestation and Parks Commission, the Director of the Department of Water Resources, and six members to be appointed by the Governor as follows: Two representatives of industries engaged in the manufacture of food products within the state, a representative of other industries within the state, a representative of agriculture within the state, and two representatives of municipal government within the state. The Governor shall make such appointments within sixty days after the effective date of this act. The members first appointed shall be so designated that their terms of office shall expire as follows: Two on the first day of March, 1958, two on the first day of March, 1959, and two on the first day of March, 1960. In 1958 and each year thereafter the Governor shall appoint members to fill the vacancies resulting from the expiration of the terms of the members of the council occurring in such year. Each such appointment shall be for a three year term from and after the first day of March in the year in which such appointment is made. Any vacancy on the council shall be filled by the Governor by the appointment of a qualified person for the unexpired term; and

(b) A chairman shall be elected by the members of the council from among the appointed members. The council shall also elect from its appointed members a vice-chairman who shall hold office at the pleasure of the council and shall serve as chairman in case of the absence or disability of the chairman.

(2) No compensaton shall be allowed any member of the council for services rendered as a member thereof; all members of the council shall be reimbursed by the department for travel and other necessary expenses incurred in their official duties as members of the council, except that expenses of members who are in the employ of the state shall be paid from the appropriation to their respective departments;

(3) The council shall hold at least two regular meetings each calendar year at a time and place fixed by the council and shall keep a record of its proceedings which shall be open to the public for inspection. Special meetings may be called by the chairman. Such special meetings must be called by him upon receipt of a written request signed by two or more members of the council. Written notice of the time and place of all meetings shall be mailed in advance to the office of each member of the council by the secretary. The majority of the members of the council shall constitute a quorum;

(4) The secretary shall be selected by the council. The secretary shall keep all records of meetings of and actions taken by the council. He shall keep the council advised as to actions taken by persons in response to recommendations and orders issued under the authority of this act. He shall perform other duties as determined by the council, not inconsistent with rules, regulations, and policies adopted by the council under the authority of this act or specific authority otherwise given by the council; and

(5) The council shall employ, with the approval of appropriate state officials, such legal, technical, scientific, or other personnel as may be necessary for the discharge of its duties under this act.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3003 (Cum. Supp. 1970)

Sec. 4

In addition to other powers and duties herein conferred and imposed upon the council, it shall have and exercise the following powers and duties under this act:

(1) To hold hearings, administer oaths, subpoena witnesses, and take testimony in all matters arising under the provisions of this act as herein provided;

(2) To formulate, review, and adopt comprehensive pro grams for the prevention, control, and abatement of pollu tion of the waters of the state as developed in accordance with the provisions of this act;

(3) To adopt, modify, or repeal after hearing standards of quality of effluent of disposal systems and standards of quality of the waters of the state;

(4) To classify the waters of the state in accordance with the provisions of this act; and

(5) To issue, modify, or revoke orders, after appropriate hearing, regulating te discharge of waste in violation of standards of quality of effluents as established by the council.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3004 (Cum. Supp. 1970)

Sec. 5

(1) It is recognized that due to variable factors no single standard of quality of waters is applicable to all waters of the state or to different segments of the same waters;

(2) In order to attain the objectives of this act the council, after proper study and conducting public hearings upon due notice, may group the designated waters of the state into classes. Such classification shall be made in accordance with consideration of the best interests of the state and of the persons in the area affected by such classification and with regard to the consideration mentioned in subsection (3) hereof. Such elassification may from time to time be altered or modified by the council after a hearing thereon;

(3) In adopting the classification of waters and the standards of quality above mentioned, consideration shall be given to:

(a) The size, depth, surface area covered, volume, direction and rate of flow, stream gradient, and temperature of the water;

(b) The character of the area affected by such classification or standards, its peculiar suitability for particular purposes, conserving the value of the same, and encouraging the most appropriate use of lands within such area for domestic, agricultural, industrial, or recreational purposes;

(c) The uses which have been made, are being made, or are likely to be made, of said waters for agricultural, transportation, domestic and industrial consumption, fishing and fish culture, fire prevention, the disposal of sewage, industrial waste and other wastes, or other uses within this state and, at the discretion of the council, any such uses in another state on interstate waters flowing through or originating in this state; and

(d) The extent of present defilement or fouling of said waters which has already occurred or resulted from past discharges therein;

(4) Prior to classifying waters and setting standards of quality or modifying or repealing such classifications or standards, the council shall conduct public hearings in connection therewith. Notice of such public hearing for the consideration, adoption, or amendment of the classification of waters and standards of quality thereof shall specify the waters concerning which a classification is sought to be made or for which standards are sought to be adopted and the time, date, and place of such hearing. Such hearing shall be held in the area to be affected by such classification or standards. Such notice shall be published at least twice in a newspaper of general circulation in the area affected. It shall also be mailed at least twenty days before such public hearing to the chief executive of each city, village, and board of county commissioners of the area affected, and may be mailed to such other persons as the council has reason to believe may be affected by such classification and the setting of such standards;

(5) The adoption of standards of quality of the waters of the state and the classification of such waters or any modification or change thereof shall be determined by the council after hearing as provided herein. Such standards or classification shall become effective when published in a newspaper of general circulation in the area affected. In classifying waters and setting standards of water quality or making any modification or change thereof, the council may establish standards of quality of effluent of disposal systems but shall determine and provide a reasonable time for persons discharging wastes into the waters of the state to comply with the classification or standards established by the council; Provided, in determining what shall constitute a reasonable time, the council shall take into consideration (a) the degree of pollution caused by the wastes of such person, (b) whether or not the wastes discharged by such person are contributing materially to the pollution of the waters involved, and (c) shall also consider the bonded indebtedness and financial status of such person; and

(6) Any discharge in accord with such classification or standards shall not be deemed to be pollution for the purposes of this act. Any city or village of the State of Nebraska which is discharging effluent in excess of the classification or standard shall not be deemed to be in violation if such city or village is engaging or does then engage in the process of collecting a sinking fund by means of a special two mill levy or raising a similar amount annually by other means for the purpose of providing the funds necessary for a sewage treatment or disposal plant.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3005 (Cum. Supp. 1970)

Sec. 6

Whenever any city or village has accumulated a sinking fund as provided in subsection (6) of section 5 of this act for a period of twenty-five years or longer and the fund is insufficient to provide a sewage treatment or disposal plant, the governing body of such city or village may terminate such fund and transfer all money belonging to it to the general fund, and such city or village shall not thereafter be deemed to be in violation of this act.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3006 (Cum. Supp. 1970)

Sec. 7

The department shall have the following powers and duties with respect to the administration of this act, subject to and consistent with the policies, programs, classifications, standards of quality, and the rules and regulations from time to time determined, established, and issued by the council:

(1) To advise, consult, and cooperate with other agencies of the state, the feeral government, other states and interstate agencies, and with affected persons, groups, political subdivisions, and industries in furtherance of the purposes of this act;

(2) To encourage, participate in, or conduct studies, investigations, research, and demonstrations relating to water pollution and causes, prevention, control, and abatement thereof;

(3) To review and make recommendations with respect to plans and specifications for disposal systems or parts thereof; and

(4) To administer such comprehensive programs for water pollution control as shall be developed by the council, and to administer, apply for, and match such federal funds as may be available.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3007 (Cum. Supp. 1970)

Sec. 8

(1) Whenever the department determines that there are reasonable grounds to believe that there has been a violation of any order of the council, written notice signed by the chairman of the council shall be served by the department on the alleged violator or violators specifying the cause of complaint. Such notice shall require that the alleged violator or violators appear before the council, or its authorized representative, at a time and place specified in the notice and answer the charges stated therein, The notice shall be served on the alleged violator or violators in accordance with the provisions of subsection (4) of this section not less than thirty days before the time set for the hearing;

(2) The council shall afford an opportunity for a fair hearing, in accordance with the provisions of section 9 of this act, to the alleged violator or violators at the time and place specified in the notice. Such hearing must be held in the county where the alleged violation or violations occurred. On the basis of the evidence produced at the hearing the council shall make due findings as justified by such evidence. It shall continue such order as may have been issued by the council in effect, revoke it, or modify it. If the hearing is held before any person other than the councii itself, such person shall transmit a record of the hearing together with recommendations to the council. It shaIl thereupon continue such order as may have been issued by the council in effect, revoke it, or modify it. A copy of the decision of the council shali be served on the alleged violator or violators. The decision of the council shall become final and binding on al1 parties, unless appealed to the courts as provided in section 10 of this act, within thirty days after service of the copy of such decision;

(3) Any city or village of the State of Nebraska, which at the time of the alleged violation is engaging or does then engage in the process of collecting a sinking fund by means of special two mill levy or in raising a similar amount annually by other means for the purpose of providing funds for construction of a sewage treatment or disposal plant, shall not be deemed to be a violator; and

(4) Except as otherwise expressly provided, any notice, order, or other instrument issued by or under authority of the council may be served on any person affected thereby personally or by publication, as provided for service of summons in a civil action. Proof of such service may be made in like manner as in case of service of a summons in a civil action. Such proof is to be filed in the office of the council. Such service may be made by mailing a copy of the notice, order, or other instrument by registered or certified mail with the postage prepaid directed to the person affected. Such copy shall be directed to his last-known address as shown by the files or records of the council. Proof thereof may be made by the affidavit of the person who did the mailing, filed in the office of the council. Every certificate or affidavit of service made and filed as herein provided shall be prima facie evidence of the facts therein stated, and a certified copy thereof shall have like force and effect.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3008 (Cum. Supp. 1970)

Sec. 9

The hearings herein provided may be conducted by the council itself at a regular or special meeting thereof or it may designate hearing officers who shall have the power and authority to conduct such hearings in the name of the council. Any such hearing may be adjourned or continued from time to time by the council or the hearing officer conducting the hearing. A record of the proceedings of said hearings shall be made and filed with the council' In any such hearing a member of the council, or a hearing officer designated by it, shall have the power to administer oaths, examine witnesses, take evidence, issue in the name of the council notice of the hearings or subpoenas requiring the testimony of witnesses and the production of evidence relevant to any matter involved in such hearing, and make recommendations upon testimony and evidence received. Witnesses who are subpoenaed shall receive the same fees and mileage as in civil actions. In case of contumacy or refusal to obey a notice of hearing or subpoena issued under this section, the district court shall have jurisdiction, upon application of the council or its representative, to issue an order requiring such person to appear and testify or produce evidence as the case may require. Any failure to obey such order of the court may be punished by such court as contempt thereof. If requested to do so by any party concerned with said hearing, the fuII stenographic notes of the testimony presented at said hearing shall be taken and filed. The stenographer shall, upon the payment of his fees allowed by law therefor, furnish a certified transcript of the whole or any part of his notes to any party to the action requiring the same.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3009 (Cum. Supp. 1970)

Sec. 10

(1) An appeal, may be taken by any person who is or may be affected by a decision of the council or by the Attorney General on behalf of the state from any decision of the council, to the district court of the county in which such violation occurred. An appeal may also be taken, from any decision of the council classifying waters or setting standards of quality therefor, to the district court of the county in which said hearing was held by the council. Within thirty days after the effective date of a decision of the council classifying waters or setting standards of quality therefor, or within thirty days after service of a copy of the final decision of the council relating to a violation of a decision of the council, the person desiring to appeal from any such decision or his attorney shall serve a notice of appeal on the council through its secretary. During such thirty day period the proper district court may, for good cause shown, extend such time for not exceeding an additional sixty days. The notice of appeal shall refer to the decision of the council appealed from and specify the grounds of such appeal including both points of Iaw and fact which are asserted or questioned by the appellant. A copy of the original notice of appeal with proof of service shall be filed by the appellant or his attorney with the clerk of the proper district court within ten days after the service of the notice of appeal and thereupon the court shall have jurisdiction of the appeal;

(2) The appellant and the council shall in all cases be deemed the original parties to an appeal. The state through the Attorney General or any other person affected, may become a party by intervention as in a civil action upon showing cause therefor. The Attorney General shall represent the council if requested upon all such appeals unless he appeals or intervenes in behalf of the state. If the Attorney General or a member of his staff is not available to represent the council in any particular proceeding, the council is empowered to appoint and compensate special counsel including county attorneys for such proceedings, all subject to the provisions of section 84-205, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, 1943.

(3) The appeal shall be heard and determined by the court upon the issues raised by the notice of appeal. Within twenty days after service on the council of a copy of the original notice of appeal, it shall make return to said court of all documents and papers on file in the matter and all testimony taken therein. It shall also return certified copies of all its findings and decisions which shall be deemed its answer to the notice of appeal The court may, for good cause shown, extend such twenty day period for not exceeding an additional sixty days. The record of the council so filed in said court shall be returned to the council after the final disposition of the case by the district or Supreme Court. The trial of the appeal shall be de novo to the court without a jury. Upon such trial the court shall determine whether or not the council regularly pursued its authority, and whether or not the orders of the department and the decision of the council ought to be sustained. It shall also determine whether or not such orders and decision are reasonable under all of the circumstances of the case. The court may continue in effect the decision of the council or may revoke or modify it; and

(4) The council or any party who may consider himself aggrieved by a judgment entered upon the appeal of any decision of the council may have such judgment reviewed by the Supreme Court in the same manner as is provided in civil cases. Upon the final determination of such judicial proceedings, the council shall enter an order in accordance with such determination.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3010 (Cum. Supp. 1970)

Sec. 11

The Attorney General shall be the legal advisor for the council.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3011 (Cum. Supp. 1970)

Sec. 12

(1) Before construction of any disposal system by any person the plans and specifications thereof shall be submitted to the secretary of the council for review and his recommendations with respect thereto; and

(2) The council, as referred to in subsection (1) of this section, or its duly appointed agent shall have the right to enter at all reasonable times, during customary business hours, in or upon any property, public or private, for the purpose of inspecting and investigating conditions relating to pollution, which inspection or examination does not involve confidential information relating to secret processes or economics of operation.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3012 (Cum. Supp. 1970)

Sec. 13

The council is hereby authorized to require the assistance of, cooperation of, services of, and the use of the records and files in all the departments and institutions of the state. All state officers and the governing authorities of all state institutions are hereby directed to cooperate with the council in furthering the purpose of this act.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3013 (Cum. Supp. 1970)

Sec. 14

For the purposes of this act, all rules, regulations, and standards, pertaining to water pollution control of the department in force and effect immediately prior to the time this act becomes effective and not inconsistent with this act, shall remain in fuIl force and effect until superseded by rules, regulations, and standards adopted in conformance with this act.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3014 (Cum. Supp. 1970)

Sec. 15

No provision of this act shall be construed to interfere with the right to appropriate and use water as otherwise provided by law.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3015 (Cum. Supp. 1970)

Sec. 16

If any section, subsection, sentence, clause, phrase, or words of this act shall be declared invalid or unconstitutional, such determination shall not affect the validity of the remaining portions thereof.

Not codified

Sec. 17

This act may be cited as the Nebraska Water Pollution Control Act.

Not codified

Sec. 18

Since an emergency exists, this act shall be in full force and take effect, from and after its passage and approval, according to law.
