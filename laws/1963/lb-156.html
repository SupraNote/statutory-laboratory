Laws 1961, c. 404, LB156 (appr. Mar. 5, 1963).

Chapter 404

Clarification Provisions

Legislative Bill 156

Introduced by Committee on Judiciary, Donald F. McGinley, 39th District, Chairman

AN ACT to amend section 71-3005, Revised Statutes Supplement, 1961, relating to public health; to clarify the provisions thereof; and to repeal the original section.

Be it enacted by the people of the State of Nebraska,

Section 1 That section 71-3005, Revised Statutes Supplement, 1961, be amended to read as follows:

71-3005

(1) It is recognized that due to the variable factors no single standard of quality of waters is applicable to all waters of the state or to different segments of the same waters;

(2) In order to attain the objectives of sections 71-3001 to 71-3016 the council, after proper study and conducting hearings upon notice as hereinafter provided shall determine and set up standards of water quality under and by which this council, as hereinafter provided following public hearings upon due notice, may group the designated waters of the state into classes. The classifications shall be made in accordance with consideration of the best interests of the state and of the persons in the area affected by such classification and with regard to the consideration mentioned in subsection (3) hereof. Such classification may from time to time be altered or modified by the council after a hearing thereon;

(3) In adopting the classification of waters and the standards of quality above mentioned, consideration shall be given to: (a) The size, depth, surface area covered, volume, direction and rate of flow, stream gradient, and temperature of the water; (b) the character of the area affected by such classification or standards, its peculiar suitability for particular purposes, conserving the value of the same, and encouraging the most appropriate use of lands within such area for domestic, agricultural, industrial, or recreational purposes; (c) the uses which have been made, are being made, or are likely to be made, of said waters for agricultural, transportation, domestic and industrial consumption, fishing and fish culture, fire prevention, the disposal of sewage, industrial waste and other wastes, or other uses within this state and, at the discretion of the council, any such uses in another state on interstate waters flowing through or originating in this state; and (d) the extent of present defilement or fouling of said waters which has already occurred or resulted from past discharges therein;

(4) Prior to classifying waters and setting standards of quality or modifying or repealing such classifications or standards, the council shall conduct public hearings in connection therewith. Notice of such public hearing for the consideration, adoption, or amendment of the classification of waters and standards of quality thereof shall specify the waters concerning which a classification is sought to be made or for which standards are sought to be adopted and the time, date, and place of such hearing. Such hearing shall be held in the area to be affected by such classification or standards. Such notice shall be published at least twice in a newspaper of general circulation in the area affected; Provided, it shall not be necessary to publish such notice in more than one such legal newspaper in each of the Supreme Court judicial districts to be affected by such hearing. It shall also be mailed at least twenty days before such public hearing to the chief executive of each city, village, and board of county commissioners or supervisors of the area affected, and may be mailed to such other persons as the council has reason to believe may be affected by such classification and the setting of such standards;

(5) The adoption of standards of quality of the waters of the state and the classification of such waters or any modification or change thereof shall be determined by the council after hearing as provided herein. Such standards or classification shall become effective when published in a newspaper of general circulation in the area affected. In classifying waters and setting standards of water quality or making any modification or change thereof, the council may establish standards of quality of effluent of disposal systems but shall determine and provide a reasonable time for persons discharging wastes into the waters of the state to comply with the classification or standards established by the council; Provided, in determining what shall constitute a reasonable time, the council shall take into consideration (a) the degree of pollution caused by the wastes of such person, (b) whether or not the wastes discharged by such person are contributing materially to the pollution of the waters involved, and (c) shall also consider the bonded indebtedness and financial status of such person; and

(6) Any discharge in accord with such classification or standards shall not be deemed to be pollution for the purposes of sections 71-3001 to 71-3016. Any city or village of the State of Nebraska which is discharging effluent in excess of the classification or standard shall not be deemed to be in violation if such city or village: (a) Is engaging or does then engage in the process of collecting a sinking fund by means of a special two mill levy or raising a similar amount annually by some other means for the purpose of providing the funds necessary for a sewage treatment or disposal plant or interceptor sewers; (b) commences and completes construction of the facility as soon thereafter as is economically feasible. The council shall in determining the economic feasibility consider the bonded indebtedness and financial status of such city or village and said determination to be ascertained as provided for in section 71-3006; and (c) after complete compliance the governing body may transfer any balance of funds remaining to the general fund.

Sec. 2

That original section 71-3005, Revised Statutes Supplement, 1961, is repealed.

Approved March 5, 1963.
