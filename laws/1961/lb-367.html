Laws 1961, LB 367, c. 345, pp. 1098–1103 (appr. Apr. 12, 1961).

Chapter 345

Water Pollution Control

Legislative Bill 367

Introduced by George Syas, 4th District; Ross H. Rasmussen, 11th District

AN ACT to amend section 71-3003, 71-3005, and 71-3006, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, 1943, relating to water pollution control; to provide for a per diem for members of the state water pollution control council as prescribed; to provide for setting up standards for water quality; to provide for hearings and notice; to provide for compliance in providing of a sewage treatment or disposal plant or interceptor sewers; to repeal the original sections; and to declare an emergency.

Be it enacted by the people of the State of Nebraska,

Section 1 That section 71-3003, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, 1943, be amended to read as follows:

71-3003

(1) There is hereby created within the department, a state water pollution control council, herein referred to as the council, which shall consist of nine members as follows:

(a) The Director of Health, the secretarySecretary of the Game, Forestation and Parks Commission, the Director of the Department of Water Resources, and six members to be appointed by the Governor as follows: Two representatives of industries engaged in the manufacture of food products within the state, a representative of other industries within the state, a representative of agriculture within the state, and two representatives of municipal government within the state. The Governor shall make such appointments within sixty days after the effective date of this actMay 21, 1957. The members first appointed shall be so designated that their terms of office shall expire as follows: Two on the first day of March, 1958, two on the first day of March, 1959, and two on the first day of March, 1960. In 1958 and each year thereafter the Governor shall appoint members to fill the vacancies resulting from the expiration of the terms of the members of the council occurring in such year. Each such appointment shall be for a three year term from and after the first day of March in the year in which such appointment is made. Any vacancy on the council shall be filled by the Governor by the appointment of a qualified person for the unexpired term; and

(b) A chairman shall be elected by the members of the council from among the appointed members. The council shall also elect from its appointed members a vice-chairman who shall hold office at the pleasure of the council and shall serve as chairman in case of the absence or disability of the chairman.

(2) No compensation shall be allowed any memberof the council for services rendered as a member thereof;The members of the council who are not officers or employees of the State of Nebraska while attending conferences, meetings of the council, or while otherwise serving at the request of the joint action of the council, shall receive compensation at the rate of twenty dollars per diem while so serving including travel time; all members of the council shall be reimbursed by the department for travel and other necessary expenses incurred in their official duties as members of the council, except that expenses of members who are in the employ of the state shall be paid from the appropriation to their respective departments;

(3) The council shall hold at least two regular meetings each calendar year at a time and place fixed by the council and shall keep a record of its proceedings which shall be open to the public for inspection. Special meetings may be called by the chairman. Such special meetings must be called by him upon receipt of a written request signed by two or more members of the council. Written notice of the time and place of all meetings shall be mailed in advance to the office of each member of the council by the secretary. The majority of the members of the council shall constitute a quorum;

(4) The secretary shall be selected by the council. The secretary shall keep all records of meetings of and actions taken by the council. He shall keep the council advised as to actions taken by persons in response to recommendations and orders issued under the authority of this act. He shall perform other duties as determined by the council, not inconsistent with rules, regulations, and policies adopted by the council under the authority of this act or specific authority otherwise given by the council; and

(5) The council shall employ, with the approval of appropriate state officials, such legal, technical, scientific, or other personnel as may be necessary for the discharge of its duties under this act.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3003

Sec. 2 That section 71-3005, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, 1943, be amended to read as follows:

71-3005

(1) It is recognized that due to the variable factors no single standard of quality of waters is applicable to all waters of the state or to different segments of the same waters;

(2) In order to attain the objectives of this act the council, after proper study and conducting hearings upon notice as hereinafter provided shall determine and set up standards of water quality under and by which this council, as hereinafter provided following public hearings upon due notice, may group the designated waters of the state into classes. The classifications shall be made in accordance with consideration of the best interests of the state and of the persons in the area affected by such classification and with regard to the consideration mentioned in subsection (3) hereof. Such classification may from time to time be altered or modified by the council after a hearing thereon;

(3) In adopting the classification of waters and the standards of quality above mentioned, consideration shall be given to: (a) The size, depth, surface area covered, volume, direction and rate of flow, stream gradient, and temperature of the water; (b) the character of the area affected by such classification or standards, its peculiar suitability for particular purposes, conserving the value of the same, and encouraging the most appropriate use of lands within such area for domestic, agricultural, industrial, or recreational purposes; (c) the uses which have been made, are being made, or are likely to be made, of said waters for agricultural, transportation, domestic and industrial consumption, fishing and fish culture, fire prevention, the disposal of sewage, industrial waste and other wastes, or other uses within this state and, at the discretion of the council, any such uses in another state on interstate waters flowing through or originating in this state; and (d) the extent of present defilement or fouling of said waters which has already occurred or resulted from past discharges therein;

(4) Prior to classifying waters and setting standards of quality or modifying or repealing such classifications or standards, the council shall conduct public hearings in connection therewith. Notice of such public hearing for the consideration, adoption, or amendment of the classification of waters and standards of quality thereof shall specify the waters concerning which a classification is sought to be made or for which standards are sought to be adopted and the time, date, and place of such hearing. Such hearing shall be held in the area to be affected by such classification or standards. Such notice shall be published at least twice in a newspaper of general circulation in the area affected.; Provided, it shall not be necessary to publish such notice in more than one such legal newspaper in each of the Supreme Court judicial districts to be affected by such hearing. It shall also be mailed at least twenty days before such public hearing to the chief executive of each city, village, and board of county commissioners of the area affected, and may be mailed to such other persons as the council has reason to believe may be affected by such classification and the setting of such standards;

(5) The adoption of standards of quality of the waters of the state and the classification of such waters or any modification or change thereof shall be determined by the council after hearing as provided herein. Such standards or classification shall become effective when published in a newspaper of general circulation in the area affected. In classifying waters and setting standards of water quality or making any modification or change thereof, the council may establish standards of quality of effluent of disposal systems but shall determine and provide a reasonable time for persons discharging wastes into the waters of the state to comply with the classification or standards established by the council; Provided, in determining what shall constitute a reasonable time, the council shall take into consideration (a) the degree of pollution caused by the wastes of such person, (b) whether or not the wastes discharged by such person are contributing materially to the pollution of the waters involved, and (c) shall also consider the bonded indebtedness and financial status of such person; and

(6) Any discharge in accord with such classification or standards shall not be deemed to be pollution for the purposes of this act. Any city or village of the State of Nebraska which is discharging effluent in excess of the classification or standard shall not be deemed to be in violation if such city or village: (a) is Is engaging or does then engage in the process of collecting a sinking fund by means of a special two mill levy or raising a similar amount annually by some other means for the purpose of providing the funds necessary for a sewage treatment or disposal plant. or interceptor sewers; (b) commences and completes construction of the facility as soon thereafter as is economically feasible. The council shall in determining the economic feasibility consider the bonded indebtedness and financial status of such city or village and said determination to be ascertained as provided for in section 71-3006; and (c) after complete compliance the governing body may transfer any balance of funds remaining to the general fund.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3005

Sec. 3 That section 71-3006, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, 1943, be amended to read as follows:

71-3006

Whenever any city or village has accumulated a sinking fund as provided in subsection (6) of section 5 of this act for a period of twenty-five years or longer and the fund is insufficient to provide a sewage treatment or disposal plant, the governing body of such city or village may terminate such fund and transfer all money belonging to it to the general fund, and such city or village shall not thereafter be deemed to be in violation of this act.If a city or village which is discharging its effluent in excess of the classification or standard has for over three years after said classification or determination of standard complied with subdivision (a) of subsection (6) of section 71-3005, the control council may determine, set and order such hearing as shall be necessary to determine whether the city or village has complied with subdivision (b) of subsection (6) of section 71-3005, and after due notice of not less than thirty days and hearing shall determine whether it is economically feasible and if so, shall order compliance.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 71-3006

Sec. 4

That original sections, 71-3003, 71-3005, and 71-3006, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, 1943, are repealed.

Not codified
