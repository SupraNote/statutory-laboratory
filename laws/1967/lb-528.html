Chapter 158

Unlawful Accumulation of Junk

Legislative Bill 528

Introduced by Edward R. Danner, 11th District; Fern Hubbard Orme, 29th District; Leslie Robinson, 36th District; Fred W. Carstens, 30th District; harold T. Moylan, 6th District; Bill K. Bloom, 20th District

AN ACT relating to crimes and punishments; to define a term; to provide that it shall be unlawful to permit the accumulation of junk; to provide duties; and to provide penalties.

Original URL; Archived URL

Be it enacted by the people of the State of Nebraska,

Section 1 As used in this act, unless the context otherwise requires, junk shall mean old scrap, copper, brass, iron, steel, rope, rags, batteries, paper, trash, rubber debris, waste, dismantled or wrecked automobiles, or parts thereof, and other old or scrap ferrous or nonferrous material.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 28-1035 (Cum. Supp. 1970)

Sec. 2 It shall be unlawful for any property owner to allow the accumulation of junk on property that is not purely agricultural in character to the extent that such accumulation is a potential hazard to health.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 28-1036 (Cum. Supp. Oct. 1970)

Sec. 3 The state or city Department of Health, as the case may be, shall have the power to investigate all complaints of violations of this act and, if the department finds that the property owner has allowed such an accumulation, shall give notice to the owner by certified or registered mail to remove the accumulation within thirty days.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 28-1037 (Cum. Supp. Oct. 1970)

Sec. 4 When any property owner fails or refuses to remove an accumulation of junk as directed by the Department of Health pursuant to section 3 of this act, the department shall ask the county attorney of the county in which the property is locateed to prosecute the property owner for violation of section 2 of this act.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 28-1038 (Cum. Supp. Oct. 1970)

Sec. 5 Any property owner violating the provisions of this act shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall, upon conviction thereof, be punished by a fine of not less than twenty-five dollars nor more than one hundred dollars, and the court, as a part of the judgment of conviction, shall order the owner to remove the accumulation of junk.

Neb. Rev. Stat. § 28-1039 (Cum. Supp. Oct. 1970)
